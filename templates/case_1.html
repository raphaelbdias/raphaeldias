CASE STUDY


A case study of a retail company, referred to as "Sample Superstore," was conducted to analyze the firm's sales and customer data to improve business performance and decision-making.
The study utilized various data visualization tools and statistical analysis techniques to identify key trends and insights within the company's operations.


This trend of a general dip in sales during the first quarter can be seen throughout the years from 2014 to 2017. Despite this drop, the overall trend still shows an upward slope for quarterly sales and profit during this time period. The highest peak was observed in 2016 Q4, while the lowest drop was in 2015 Q1.




A map chart of the USA that shows sales by color would indicate the sales performance of different states using a color coding system. Red color would represent low sales and green color would represent high sales. By looking at the map, one can quickly identify which states have low and high sales, and make data-driven decisions accordingly. The map chart can be interactive and allow users to hover over each state to view the exact sales figures. This type of visualization is useful for getting a quick and intuitive understanding of the sales performance of different states in the USA, and is particularly useful for businesses looking to make decisions about where to focus their marketing efforts or where to expand their operations.




The highlight table shows sales by category and segment using color shade to indicate profit. Darker shade means higher profit. This visualization allows quick identification of trends and patterns, making informed decisions easier. The color shading provides a visual cue to relative value. A highlight table helps organizations understand sales performance quickly.




A bubble chart is a type of data visualization that uses circles (bubbles) to represent data points. In this case, the sub-categories of sales are represented by the size of the bubbles, and the profit level is represented by the color, with red representing low profit and green representing high profit. This type of chart allows users to see the relationship between two variables, size and color, and understand the distribution of data in a clear and concise manner. The bubble chart helps to identify trends and patterns in sales and profit, allowing organizations to make informed decisions about their business strategy.
